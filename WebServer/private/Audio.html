Sound and audio processing


Sound transfer energy along acoustic waves. It uses the particles in the air and therefore sound cannot travel through a vacuum, which is somewhere with no air. Sound waves have the characteristics of amplitude, which is the intensity of the wave, and is related to how loud the sound is and frequency this changes the pitch of the sound.


Audio processing is saved as an WAv files. The audio contains a sample rate and a resolution. Bite-rates are the number of bits proceeds per second. Bit rate = sample rate x resolution. We can compress the amount of data to minimise the amount of data in the form of MP3. This reduces the bit rate to reduce the file size. It does this by processing the threshold of hearing and masking.
